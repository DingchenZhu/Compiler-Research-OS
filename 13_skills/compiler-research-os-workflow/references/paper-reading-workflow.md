# Paper Reading Workflow

## Purpose

Use this for reading compiler, GPU, architecture, and AI systems papers.

## Reading Levels

```text
Skim: title, abstract, figures, claims, limitations
Normal: problem, method, evidence, related work
Deep: reproduce core claim, map to source/experiment/architecture
```

## Required Output

Every serious paper note should include:

- one-line summary
- problem
- key idea
- method
- evidence
- assumptions
- limitations
- compiler architecture relevance
- reproduction plan
- follow-up ideas

## Compiler-Specific Questions

Ask:

- What IR abstraction does this assume?
- What hardware model does it use?
- What scheduling or memory decision is central?
- What is measured, and what is not measured?
- Which claim can I reproduce locally?
- Which part could become a prototype?

## Link Targets

Connect paper notes to:

- concepts in `10_knowledge/concepts/`
- experiments in `05_experiments/`
- ideas in `04_research/ideas/`
- source reading in `03_source_reading/`
- outputs in `08_outputs/`

## Completion Standard

A paper is not “read” until it has:

- limitation section
- reproduction plan
- at least one related concept or research idea
