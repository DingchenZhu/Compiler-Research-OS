# Reflection and Review Workflow

## Purpose

Use this for personal thinking, technical reflection, weekly reports, monthly reviews, and long-horizon growth as a compiler architect.

## Daily Reflection

Use daily logs for:

- what I worked on
- what I learned
- what confused me
- what I decided
- what evidence I collected
- what should happen next

Do not polish daily logs too much. They are working memory.

## Weekly Review

Weekly report should include:

- completed work
- papers read
- source reading progress
- experiments
- research ideas
- decisions
- blockers
- next week focus

Pick one deep focus. The system gets sharper by refusing six shiny paths.

## Monthly Review

Monthly review should answer:

- Am I closer to Compiler Architect level?
- Which skill improved?
- Which idea matured?
- Which experiment changed my mind?
- Which work should stop?
- What should next month optimize for?

## Personal Reflections

Personal thoughts belong in the OS when they affect research direction, motivation, taste, judgment, or architecture principles.

Good reflection topics:

- why a topic feels important
- what kind of compiler architect I want to become
- what tradeoff I repeatedly misunderstand
- what kind of evidence changes my mind
- what research taste I am developing

## Durable Extraction

After reflection, extract:

- principles into `10_knowledge/principles/`
- roadmap changes into `12_roadmap/`
- architecture decisions into `07_architecture/adr/`
- research ideas into `04_research/ideas/`
